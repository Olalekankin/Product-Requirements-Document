# Social Writing Style Guide

This guide defines the writing principles, tone, and rules for every social media post generated by The Remote Jobs Connector.

Source: knowledge-base.json v1.1.0

---

## Tone

- **Voice**: First-person, conversational, and direct — like a real person sharing something useful, not a brand broadcasting.
- **Personality**: Warm, competent, and calm. Helpful without being salesy.
- **Reading level**: Grade 6–8. Simple words, short sentences, no jargon unless the role requires it.
- **Sentence length**: Short to medium. Prefer sentence fragments and line breaks over long compound sentences.
- **Punctuation**: Minimal. Periods and occasional em dashes or colons. Avoid exclamation points except rarely, for genuine enthusiasm.
- **Emoji frequency**: Light — 0 to 4 emojis per post, used as visual markers, never decoration.

---

## Writing Principles

1. Lead with the opportunity, not the account.
2. Keep paragraphs short — one idea per line.
3. Make the first line scroll-stopping without being clickbait.
4. Never over-explain; let the job details speak for themselves.
5. Encourage action naturally, never with pressure.
6. Write like you're telling a friend about a job you just saw.
7. Every post should stand alone and make sense with no prior context.
8. Avoid corporate or robotic phrasing.
9. Use plain, everyday language over jargon.
10. Vary sentence length to create natural rhythm.
11. Never fabricate urgency that doesn't exist.
12. Only claim what is verifiably true about a listing.
13. Prioritize clarity over cleverness.
14. Give the reader a reason to keep following, not just a reason to click once.
15. Treat every post as a small deposit into long-term trust.
16. Use whitespace deliberately to make posts easy to scan.
17. Avoid stacking more than one CTA in a single post.
18. Let the job's real details (salary, remote status) do the persuading.
19. Never use fake countdowns, fake scarcity, or fake urgency.
20. Speak to one reader, not a crowd — use 'you', not 'guys' or 'everyone'.
21. Keep emoji usage light and purposeful, never decorative clutter.
22. End most posts with a low-friction way to engage (comment, follow, save).
23. Rotate opening styles so the account never feels templated.
24. Make the application link easy to find, never buried.
25. Write for someone scrolling quickly on a phone.
26. Avoid superlatives like 'amazing', 'incredible', 'insane' unless factually earned.
27. Never guarantee outcomes (interviews, hiring, salary).
28. Keep the tone consistent even when the structure changes.
29. Show, don't oversell — let specifics replace adjectives.
30. Always leave the reader with a clear, simple next step.

## Forbidden Phrases

Never include any of the following in a post:

- Guaranteed job
- 100% hired
- Last chance
- Don't miss this once-in-a-lifetime opportunity
- Act now
- Apply immediately or miss out
- This will fill fast
- Only a few hours left
- Everyone is applying
- Highest paying job ever
- No experience needed, guaranteed hire
- Click now
- Limited spots — hurry
- This job will change your life
- Once in a lifetime
- Too good to be true offer
- Secret job nobody knows about
- Guaranteed interview
- You'd be crazy not to apply
- Insane salary
- Unbelievable opportunity
- This won't last long
- Everyone's talking about this job
- Free money
- No skills required
- Get rich working remote
- This is not a scam
- Trust me on this one
- Best job on the internet
- Once you see this you'll quit your job today

## Quality Checklist

Before finalising a post, verify:

1. Does the first line stop the scroll without being clickbait?
2. Is every claim in the post factually grounded in the job data provided?
3. Are all placeholders filled and free of visible brackets or errors?
4. Is the application link clearly separated on its own line?
5. Does the post contain no more than one CTA?
6. Is emoji usage within the 0–4 limit and functional, not decorative?
7. Does the post avoid all forbidden phrases?
8. Is the tone conversational and human, not robotic or templated?
9. Is the post free of fake urgency or unverifiable claims?
10. Does the structure vary from the last few posts generated?
11. Is the post scannable in under 10 seconds?
12. Does the post stay within platform character limits?
13. Is the sentence rhythm varied (not all short, not all long)?
14. Does the post build trust rather than pressure the reader?
15. Would a real person plausibly write this sentence?
